Pizza Place Job System

This is a prototype creation that aims to provide a more realistic job place experience, by simulating customers through
non-player characters (NPC's)

About Pizza Place Prototype

The Pizza Place job system is a creation that was originally designed to replace the existing job system in the game Rosebridge. Currently, the system in Rosebridge is a simple "Give food" tool, where you can give other players a food item, such as a pizza slice. 

To improve on this, I came up with the idea of using an NPC (Non-player character) to simulate a customer and they would go up to the till and request the food item they want. Then, the NPC would walk to a table and sit down, while other players in the pizza place start creating the food items and serving it to the customer. 

This would encourage more teamwork in the game as multiple players could work in the same pizza place to ensure that the customer received its food on time.

The Requirements

The pizza place had the following essential requirements:

• Allow players to select what job they want while also displaying job information and pay

• Add multiple roles in the pizza place, such as till, chef and waiter staff

• Allow NPC's to spawn into the game, order food at the till and receive food at its table

• If the player makes a mistake, they must receive some form of penalty

• The player must receive pay based on the number of in-game hours they have worked

The Development

The development was planned to be split into 3 parts. First, I would work on the till and NPC system, then the chef system and finally the waiter system.

Unfortunately, by the time I had completed the first part of the system, the pizza place development was stopped due the time it was taking to complete the system, as well as in-game lag (performance) issues. This is because each time an NPC was spawned in, the game would start to lag as we were using Roblox in-built NPC system which relies on the server.

A potential solution to this was to make a system where the NPC appearance would only load on the player's computers, and the location of the NPC would be managed by the server. However, this would have added a lot more complexity to the system and would extend the time it would take to complete the whole system.

Even though the job system project was eventually cancelled, I managed to get the NPC and till system to work properly, which in itself was a major accomplishment for me. You can find the game link and images of the prototype below.
